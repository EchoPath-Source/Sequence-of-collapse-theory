# Claim Boundaries for Product Use

## Purpose

This guide defines how SoCT concepts may be referenced by product, architecture, and public-facing repositories without overstating the empirical status of Sequence of Collapse Theory.

The safe rule is simple:

> Product repositories may use SoCT-inspired engineering language only after the concept has been translated into a product-safe primitive that does not require unproven physics to be true.

## Claim tiers

### Tier 0 — Internal inspiration

Use for concepts that are poetic, metaphysical, speculative, or early-stage theoretical.

Allowed language:

- inspired by collapse-history memory,
- field-inspired,
- memory-field-inspired,
- coherence-inspired,
- recursive memory metaphor,
- conceptual scaffold.

Do not use for product performance claims.

### Tier 1 — Implemented engineering primitive

Use when a product repo has implemented a real feature independent of whether SoCT is physically true.

Allowed language:

- adaptive memory,
- spatial memory,
- event persistence,
- replay-based tuning,
- memory-biased routing,
- threshold-triggered behavior,
- field-inspired diagnostics,
- topology-aware scoring.

### Tier 2 — Reproducible research result

Use when a result has a stable dataset, method, script/notebook, outputs, and repeatable checks.

Allowed language:

- preliminary evidence,
- reproduced internal result,
- controlled simulation result,
- preregistered analysis plan,
- reproducibility packet available.

### Tier 3 — Publication-grade claim

Use only after external-ready methods, data, analysis, limitations, and citations are present.

Allowed language depends on the specific paper and peer-review status.

## Product-safe language

Product repos may use:

- adaptive memory,
- persistent spatial memory,
- simulation memory,
- event-history field,
- field-inspired routing,
- memory-biased pathing,
- coherence-inspired stability metric,
- topology-aware diagnostics,
- replay-derived analytics.

## Product language to avoid

Product repos should not claim:

- proven new physics,
- experimentally confirmed memory fields,
- consciousness-driven collapse,
- dark matter replacement,
- dark energy replacement,
- quantum hardware validation,
- neuromorphic hardware readiness,
- biological equivalence,
- physical Source/Origin verification,
- evidence of consciousness substrate,
- product performance because SoCT is true.

## Examples

### Unsafe

> EchoPath proves that space has memory.

### Safe

> EchoPath gives simulated spaces persistent event memory, so rooms, paths, and agents can respond to accumulated history.

### Unsafe

> Witness-Q validates quantum hardware using Sequence of Collapse physics.

### Safe

> Witness-Q is a proposed research direction for topology-aware validation of event-driven and coherence-sensitive systems using memory-field simulations and bounded engineering metrics.

### Unsafe

> CNN-SoCT proves consciousness collapse.

### Safe

> CNN-SoCT is an internal research model for studying event-driven memory-field dynamics, criticality, recursive compression, and anomaly detection under controlled simulation settings.

## Required disclaimer for research-derived product notes

When a product-facing doc references SoCT research inspiration, include a boundary note:

> This product language is engineering-facing and does not claim experimental proof of Sequence of Collapse Theory, new physics, consciousness-driven collapse, or physical memory fields.

## Final rule

Use SoCT as the research furnace. Use product repos as the practical surface. Do not collapse the two claim layers into one public statement.
