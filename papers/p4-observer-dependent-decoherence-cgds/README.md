# P4 / CGDS-01 — Observer-Dependent Decoherence & Consciousness-Gated Quantum Experiments

## Purpose

This folder is the paper and protocol hub for **P4** in the Sequence of Collapse Theory publication sequence.

It upgrades the original **CGDS-01: Consciousness-Gated Double-Slit Experiment** into a broader quantum-observer research track investigating whether apparent collapse differs when measurement information is merely recorded by a machine versus when that information becomes accessible to conscious observation.

The central working question is:

> Does conscious access to measurement information act as a finalizing boundary condition for collapse, beyond ordinary detector interaction and environmental decoherence?

CGDS-01 remains the anchor experiment. This P4 hub also tracks SOC-MZI, delayed-choice/quantum-eraser reinterpretations, awareness-conditioned quantum computing, and quantum brain-field coupling proposals.

## Core hypothesis

The Sequence of Collapse framework proposes a layered collapse process:

1. **Pre-collapse / primordial state** — unresolved quantum potential.
2. **First collapse / photonic or detector interaction** — decoherence and initial localization.
3. **Second collapse / conscious access** — finalization into experienced outcome.

Under this model, detector interaction may create physical records and alter interference, but P4 asks whether final outcome-selection is completed only when measurement information enters conscious-accessible experience.

## CGDS-01 anchor protocol

The original CGDS protocol compares double-slit conditions in which which-path information is:

- measured and consciously reviewed by human observers;
- measured and stored but not reviewed during the experimental window;
- measured but deleted or rendered inaccessible before conscious access;
- compared against standard no-path-information controls.

The intended observables include interference visibility, fringe contrast, Fourier-domain structure, and condition-wise deviations under controlled detector/decoherence regimes.

## Experimental branches

### 1. Consciousness-Gated Double-Slit (CGDS)

Tests whether human access to which-path data modulates interference beyond detector interaction alone.

### 2. SOC-MZI / Mach-Zehnder interferometer track

Extends the observer-access question to interferometer systems where path recombination, phase manipulation, and which-path erasure can be more tightly controlled than in the standard double-slit framing.

### 3. Delayed-choice / quantum-eraser reinterpretation

Examines whether apparent retrocausal effects can be reframed as delayed finalization of information into conscious-accessible records rather than literal backward-in-time causation.

### 4. Awareness-conditioned quantum computing

Explores whether conscious observation, meditative attention, distracted attention, or blinded/no-access protocols correlate with decoherence rates, output distributions, or error patterns in quantum computational systems.

### 5. Quantum brain-field coupling

A downstream exploratory branch testing whether neural coherence, attention state, or subjective focus correlates with quantum-system outputs under controlled and blinded conditions.

## Falsification and caution language

P4 is intended to be testable and falsifiable. The strongest negative result would be no reliable difference between conscious-access, inaccessible-record, deleted-record, and machine-only conditions after controlling for detector effects, environmental decoherence, experimental drift, publication bias, and subjective-state confounds.

This folder should preserve the speculative origin of the idea while progressively converting it into preregistered, controlled, physics-facing experimental language.
