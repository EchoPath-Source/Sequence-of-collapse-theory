# SOC-CGDS-01 / SOC-MZI-01 — Consciousness-Gated Double-Slit Experiment

**Status:** Pre-registered / ready for implementation  
**OSF:** https://osf.io/mxt7h  
**Framework:** Sequence of Collapse Theory  
**Claim level:** adversarial experimental test; not evidence until implemented and replicated

## Short Summary

Adversarial test of whether conscious access is required for final wave function collapse, distinguishing it from decoherence, physical detection, and ordinary measurement.

## Purpose

SOC-CGDS-01 tests the staged collapse model by separating physical interaction from conscious access to measurement information.

The Sequence of Collapse framework distinguishes:

```text
First Collapse  = photon-mediated interaction / decoherence / physical narrowing
Second Collapse = conscious access / experiential completion / final registration
```

The experiment asks whether path information that is physically measured but never consciously accessed behaves differently from path information that enters conscious awareness.

## Core Hypothesis

If SoCT's staged-collapse model is correct, then conscious access may contribute a distinct finalization channel beyond ordinary detector interaction.

Conservative hypothesis:

> Conscious access to which-path information may alter interference visibility or coherence statistics beyond detector-only and inaccessible-record controls.

## Null Hypothesis

Standard quantum theory and decoherence predict that physical measurement, entanglement with apparatus, and environmental information leakage are sufficient to destroy interference. Conscious access should not add an independent effect once physical measurement is controlled.

## Experimental Logic

Compare conditions such as:

```text
A. Which-path information recorded and consciously accessed.
B. Which-path information recorded but never consciously accessed.
C. Which-path information recorded and destroyed or encrypted beyond access.
D. Detector-only / observer-absent controls.
```

The key distinction is not whether a detector exists, but whether measurement information becomes available to conscious experience.

## Connection to SoCT

This directly tests the staged collapse model:

```text
Primordial potential
    -> photon / matter interaction
    -> decoherence / first narrowing
    -> conscious access
    -> final experiential collapse
```

If no difference appears between conscious-access and non-access conditions, the strong consciousness-gated version of SoCT is constrained.

## Falsification / Constraint Conditions

The consciousness-gated claim is weakened if:

1. blinded trials show no difference between conscious-access and non-access conditions,
2. any apparent difference is fully explained by detector configuration, environmental noise, thermal drift, vibration, data handling, or selection bias,
3. effects fail preregistered analysis thresholds,
4. independent replication fails.

## Implementation Notes

This experiment must be designed adversarially:

- blinded or double-blinded analysis where possible,
- preregistered primary outcome variable,
- detector and environment controls,
- no post-hoc subgroup rescue,
- full data/code release,
- independent replication encouraged.

## Paper-Safe Language

Use:

> SOC-CGDS-01 is a preregistered adversarial test of whether conscious access contributes to final collapse beyond physical measurement and decoherence.

Avoid:
