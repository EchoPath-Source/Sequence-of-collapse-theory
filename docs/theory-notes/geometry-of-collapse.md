## 2. Core Operators

### Compression

Compression is the movement of information, energy, structure, or possibility into a smaller or more constrained state.

From below, compression is experienced as pressure, density, stress, curvature, conflict, or limitation.

From above, compression may appear as organization, refinement, or preparation for emergence.

### Closure

Closure is the formation of a bounded system. A system must close enough to preserve identity, accumulate memory, and distinguish inside from outside.

Closure appears as:

- color neutrality in hadrons,
- molecular bonding,
- membrane formation,
- neural attractor states,
- event horizons,
- or causal boundaries.

### Saturation

Saturation occurs when a closed system approaches its containment capacity. Additional compression can no longer be absorbed without changing the system’s state.

Saturation is the pre-threshold condition.

### Threshold

Threshold is the point where the existing geometry cannot continue to contain the compressed state without transformation.

Threshold is where hidden bridge-degrees become active.

### Bridge Degree

A bridge degree is a hidden or latent variable that allows a lower layer to become a higher layer.

It is not always a literal spatial dimension. It may appear as:

- phase,
- orientation,
- charge,
- spin,
- valence,
- chirality,
- topology,
- memory,
- coherence,
- or compression depth.

### Through

Through is the emergent direction that opens when a closed system can no longer resolve itself inside its existing geometry.

Through is not simply another path inside the same space. It is the new mode of resolution that appears after threshold.

### Projection

Projection is the visible lower-dimensional expression of a deeper structure.

A quark may be a projection endpoint of hidden-dimensional color orientation. A particle may be a projection of field excitation. A thought may be a projection of neural and memory-field dynamics.

### Folding

Folding is the containment of higher-dimensional or higher-order structure inside a lower apparent form.

Planck cells may be folded compactified interiors. DNA folds biological memory. Neural networks fold experience into weights and attractors.

### Unfolding

Unfolding is the release of hidden geometry into an accessible layer or direction.

The Big Bang may be a Planck-cell-like unfolding. Black-hole bounce may be an inside-to-outside unfolding. A new idea may be cognitive unfolding from compressed memory.

### Memory

Memory is the persistence of prior collapse events as structure, tendency, residue, or field influence.

Memory is what makes collapse cumulative rather than isolated.

### Residue

Residue is what remains after a system attempts to resolve, collapse, transmit, or unfold but cannot fully erase the prior state.

Residue may appear as:

- entanglement modification,
- hysteresis,
- anomaly,
- gravitational memory,
- neural trace,
- phase lag,
- or compression cost.

### Correction

Correction is the process by which a system redistributes, shrinks, concentrates, or unfolds unresolved residue.

Correction determines whether compression stabilizes, avalanches, phase-transitions, collapses, or unfolds.
