# From Kerr to Cosmos

Source document: `kerr_to_cosmos.docx`.

## Thesis
This note extends the Causal Inversion Hypothesis by proposing that our universe was born from the collapse of a rotating Kerr black hole in a parent universe, and that the parent angular-momentum axis survives as a preferred direction in the child universe.

## Main quantitative claim preserved from the draft
The source manuscript derives a mapping between the parent black hole spin parameter `a/M` and the observed CMB hemispherical power asymmetry amplitude `A`.

Using the draft's preferred scaling,
- observed `A = 0.066 +/- 0.021`
- inferred parent spin `a/M ~ 0.26`

## Physical picture
- Schwarzschild collapse -> isotropic child universe
- Kerr collapse -> child universe with a built-in directional asymmetry
- the so-called Axis of Evil is interpreted as a fossil of the parent collapse axis

## Why this matters in-repo
This note is one of the clearest examples of SoCT / CIH trying to turn a broad cosmological intuition into a falsifiable quantitative claim.

## Tests preserved from the source draft
The manuscript identifies multiple independent falsification routes, including:
- tighter CMB anomaly measurements
- alignment / anisotropy cross-checks
- consistency with black-hole and entropy bridge calculations

## Caution
This is a strong hypothesis note, not yet a closed observational result. It belongs in the repository as a prediction document rather than as settled theory.
