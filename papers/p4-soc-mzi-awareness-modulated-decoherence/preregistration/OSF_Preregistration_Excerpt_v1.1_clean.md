## Study Snapshot

**Purpose:** Test whether conscious access to quantum measurement outcomes is a necessary condition for wave function collapse, under adversarial controls designed to isolate consciousness from measurement, recording, and environmental noise.

## Primary Hypothesis and Outcomes

### Primary hypothesis H1

When conscious access to measurement outcomes is prevented, with verified deletion and blinding, the system will exhibit a statistically detectable difference in interference/collapse-relevant metrics compared to matched control conditions where conscious access is allowed.

### Primary outcome measure

A pre-specified collapse proxy derived from the experiment's measurement stream, such as interference visibility, parity signal, or another device-specific collapse metric. The exact metric definition, units, and computation procedure must be fixed in the analysis plan.

## Control Logic

The protocol is designed to distinguish:

1. machine measurement;
2. stored but inaccessible records;
3. deleted or rendered-inaccessible records;
4. conscious-access conditions;
5. standard environmental and detector decoherence.

The core adversarial question is:

> Is conscious access distinguishable from ordinary recording and decoherence after blinding and deletion controls?
