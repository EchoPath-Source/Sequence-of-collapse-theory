# P4 / CGDS-01 — Observer-Dependent Decoherence & Consciousness-Gated Quantum Experiments

## Overview

This project is the P4 quantum-observer hub for the Sequence of Collapse Theory (SoCT). It upgrades the original CGDS-01 Consciousness-Gated Double-Slit Experiment into a broader experimental program investigating whether quantum decoherence and apparent collapse differ when path information is merely recorded by a machine versus when that information becomes accessible to conscious observation.

The central question is:

**Does conscious access to measurement information act as a finalizing boundary condition for collapse, beyond ordinary detector interaction and environmental decoherence?**

CGDS-01 remains the anchor experiment, while the P4 hub also tracks SOC-MZI, delayed-choice/quantum-eraser reinterpretations, awareness-conditioned quantum computing, and quantum brain-field coupling proposals.

## Core hypothesis

The Sequence of Collapse framework proposes a layered collapse process:

1. **Pre-collapse / Primordial state** — unresolved quantum potential.
2. **First collapse / photonic or detector interaction** — decoherence and initial localization.
3. **Second collapse / conscious access** — finalization into experienced outcome.

Under this model, a detector may create physical records and alter interference, but the theory asks whether the final collapse boundary is completed only when the measurement information is consciously accessed.

## CGDS-01 anchor experiment

The original CGDS protocol compares double-slit conditions in which which-path information is:

- measured and consciously reviewed by human observers;
- measured and stored but not reviewed during the experimental window;
- measured but deleted or rendered inaccessible before conscious access;
- compared against standard no-path-information controls.

The intended outcome measure is a change in interference visibility, fringe contrast, or related Fourier-domain structure across observer-access conditions.

## P4 experimental branches

### 1. Consciousness-Gated Double-Slit (CGDS)

Tests whether human access to which-path data modulates interference beyond detector interaction alone.

### 2. SOC-MZI / Mach-Zehnder interferometer track

Extends the same observer-access question to interferometer systems where path recombination, phase manipulation, and which-path erasure can be more tightly controlled.

### 3. Delayed-choice / quantum-eraser reinterpretation

Examines whether apparent retrocausal effects can be re-framed as delayed finalization of information into conscious-accessible records rather than literal backward-in-time causation.

### 4. Awareness-conditioned quantum computing

Explores whether real-time conscious observation, meditative attention, or distracted attention correlates with decoherence rates, outcome distributions, or error patterns in quantum computational systems.

### 5. Quantum brain-field coupling

A downstream exploratory branch testing whether neural coherence, attention state, or subjective focus correlates with quantum-system outputs under controlled conditions.
