The Threshold Catalogue lists major transition points where a system changes layer, phase, identity, or causal role.

This supports a research method for SoCT:

1. Identify the stable lower-layer object.
2. Identify the threshold condition.

## 4. Compression Tax and Residue Pressure

Thresholds may involve a cost when a system cannot remain inside its current geometry.

This can be called **compression tax**:

> Compression tax is the energetic, informational, or geometric cost paid when a system approaches or exceeds containment capacity.

Related term:

> Residue pressure is the unresolved memory or structural remainder that accumulates when compression cannot fully resolve within the current layer.

Possible examples:

- color-flux energy during quark separation,
- latent heat in phase transitions,
- entropy increase,
- avalanche onset in critical systems,
- neural overload or phase transition,
- pulsar glitches,
- black-hole formation,
- cosmological expansion pressure.

These terms may become quantitative SoCT variables later.
