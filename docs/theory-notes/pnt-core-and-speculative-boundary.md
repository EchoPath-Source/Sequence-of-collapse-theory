# Planck Nucleation Theory: Core Commitments and Speculative Extensions

**Status:** framework boundary note  
**Purpose:** distinguish the minimum working core of Planck Nucleation Theory (PNT) from later speculative extensions, analogies, and open hypotheses.

> This document does not claim empirical validation. It defines the internal architecture of PNT so the repository does not treat every downstream idea as equally foundational.

---

## 1. Core PNT Commitments

The following ideas should be treated as the current **core conceptual commitments** of PNT.

### 1.1 Planck-scale nucleation attempts

Reality is modeled as being supported by repeated Planck-scale state-formation or nucleation attempts.

A nucleation attempt is not necessarily a particle creation event. It is a proposed substrate-level attempt to establish a stable local state.

### 1.2 Success and failure channels

Nucleation attempts may stabilize or fail.

- **Successful nucleation** contributes to persistent local structure, causal continuity, or the stable vacuum substrate.
- **Failed nucleation** relaxes or returns energy/information to the underlying substrate and may contribute to an effective residual vacuum component.

The detailed microscopic mechanism remains undefined.

### 1.3 Planck cells as information-bearing local states

A Planck cell is treated as a conceptual minimum information-bearing unit or localized substrate state.

It should not automatically be interpreted as a literal rigid cubic lattice element. “Lattice” is currently shorthand for a discrete or quasi-discrete relational substrate.

### 1.4 Memory dependence

The probability, stability, or outcome of nucleation may depend on prior collapse history through the SoCT memory field.

This creates the working relation:

```text
prior collapse history → local memory state → altered future nucleation behavior
```

The exact coupling is still a model-development problem.

### 1.5 Vacuum residual / prompt exhaust hypothesis

Failed or incomplete nucleation attempts may leave a residual energetic or informational contribution.

This is the PNT candidate mechanism connected to dark-energy-like behavior. It remains a hypothesis, but it is central enough to PNT that it belongs in the core research program rather than only in peripheral speculation.

### 1.6 Environmental variation is testable

If nucleation outcomes depend on memory, collapse history, or large-scale structure, effective vacuum behavior may vary between environments such as voids, walls, and filaments.

This motivates tests such as void-versus-structure expansion or Hubble-rate differentials.

The current observational program does not establish such a differential; it tests for one.

## 2. Core Mathematical Development Targets

The following are not yet established equations, but they are the minimum quantities PNT should attempt to formalize:

- nucleation attempt rate,
- success probability,
- failed-attempt residual energy,
- memory-field dependence,
- spatial/temporal decay or transport,
- effective vacuum contribution,
- and environmental variation.

A generic scaffold is:

```text
attempt rate × failure fraction × residual per failure
→ effective local vacuum contribution
```

with the failure fraction potentially coupled to memory or collapse intensity.

## 3. Strong Extensions That Are Not Yet Core

These ideas are structurally compatible with PNT but should remain explicitly labeled **speculative extensions**.

### 3.1 Planck Cell Unfolding

A completed compactified Planck-cell-like state may unfold into a spacetime manifold.

This is a cosmogenesis extension, not required for the basic failed-nucleation/dark-energy model.

### 3.2 Black-hole child universes

Black-hole interiors may produce new causal manifolds or child universes through compression, bounce, inversion, or unfolding.

This is a major P6/P7 hypothesis and is not established by core PNT.

### 3.3 Parent-to-child memory inheritance

A child universe may inherit constants, orientation, asymmetry, or boundary information from a parent black hole or parent universe.

This is a speculative transfer mechanism.

### 3.4 Recursive Universe Model

Universes may recursively generate future universes through black-hole compression and threshold unfolding.

This is an important cosmological extension, but it is downstream of both black-hole child-universe formation and Planck-cell unfolding.

### 3.5 Higgs field as a PNT order parameter

The Higgs field may respond to or emerge from a deeper Planck-substrate phase transition.

This is not part of minimum PNT. It should be treated as a vacuum-structure extension requiring a separate closure order parameter and careful compatibility with the Standard Model.

### 3.6 Quark projection and hidden-dimensional color

Quarks may be lower-dimensional projections of deeper substrate states.

This is a particle-scale extension, not a required element of the PNT dark-energy mechanism.

### 3.7 Consciousness as a nucleation or bridge variable

Observer-state or awareness may interact with collapse or stabilization channels.

This belongs primarily to the SoCT observer/decoherence program and should not be silently imported into baseline PNT cosmology.

## 6. Current Recommended Boundary

The minimum PNT claim should be stated conservatively:

> Planck Nucleation Theory proposes that repeated substrate-scale nucleation attempts may have stable and failed channels, that prior collapse history may influence those outcomes through a memory-like field, and that residual energy from failed attempts could contribute to environment-dependent effective vacuum behavior.

Everything beyond that—including Higgs emergence, compactified unfolding, black-hole universe production, and recursive cosmology—should be presented as a named speculative extension rather than as already implied fact.
