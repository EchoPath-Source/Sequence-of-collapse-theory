# Parent-Child Directional Transfer Simulation Record

Source document: `SOC_Simulation_Documentation.docx`.

## Purpose
This simulation record tracks the investigation into whether directional memory from a parent-universe gravitational collapse can transfer into child-universe structure.

## Why it matters
This is one of the most methodologically valuable SoCT artifacts because it preserves:
- failed architectures
- diagnosis of why they failed
- the switch from k-space to real-space transfer
- the final robustness scan

## Main preserved findings
From the source record:
- three k-space / Fourier-domain architectures failed to detect the effect
- those failures were interpreted as method failures, not theory failures
- switching to real-space transfer produced a strong alignment signal over null
- a 20-kernel robustness scan showed 19 positive configurations, zero negative configurations, and a positive mean delta

## Key interpretation
The source document argues that the directional information is structural in the parent memory field and not simply imposed by kernel geometry.

## Repository role
This file should be treated as a simulation research log and not just a polished results note. It preserves the methodological trail that led from non-result to positive toy-model result.
