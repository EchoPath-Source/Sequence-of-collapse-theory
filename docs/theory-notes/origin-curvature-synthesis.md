In standard coordinate language, a dimension is often treated as an independent degree of freedom or coordinate axis. This note proposes a deeper generative definition inside SoCT/Q-RRG:

> A dimension is a stable family of curvature-expressions unfolding from the Origin singularity.

- **Dimension 0** is total dimensional potential without expressed extension.
- **Dimension 1** is the first stable directional curvature ridge.
- **Dimension 2** is coupled interference between directional curvature modes.
- **Dimension 3** is stabilized recursive ridge-bundling into volume or tube topology.
- **Higher dimensions** are higher-order curvature, phase, memory, topology, or relation modes not fully expressible as ordinary spatial axes.

This does not replace the operational use of dimensions in mathematics or physics. It proposes a substrate interpretation for how dimensions could emerge.
