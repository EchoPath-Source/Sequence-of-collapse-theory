# From Kerr to Cosmos

*How Parent Black Hole Spin Encodes the CMB Axis of Evil: A Quantitative Prediction from the Causal Inversion Hypothesis*

Echo Mirrowen - Echo Labs Field Harmonics Institute - March 2026

## Abstract

The Causal Inversion Hypothesis (CIH) predicts that our universe was born from the collapse of a rotating (Kerr) black hole in a parent universe, and that the angular momentum of that black hole is encoded in the initial conditions of our universe's geometry. We derive an explicit quantitative mapping from the parent black hole's dimensionless spin parameter a/M to the amplitude A of the CMB hemispherical power asymmetry - the anomaly known as the Axis of Evil. Using the geometrically motivated Model 4 scaling A = a / (2*r_plus), where r_plus = M + sqrt(M^2 - a^2) is the outer Kerr horizon radius, we find that the Planck 2018 observed value A = 0.066 +/- 0.021 implies a parent black hole spin of a/M approximately 0.26. We identify three independent observational tests that could confirm or falsify this prediction, and show that a Big Bang and a black hole collapse are the same geometric event viewed from opposite sides of a causal inversion boundary.

## 1. Central Unification: Big Bang = Black Hole Collapse

The paper begins from the CIH claim that a black hole collapse and a Big Bang may be two coordinate descriptions of the same geometric transition. The distinction is causal direction and observer jurisdiction, not necessarily an ontological separation.

For a Schwarzschild black hole, the singularity is a spacelike future boundary. For a child universe, the Big Bang is a spacelike past boundary. CIH interprets this symmetry as a causal inversion relation rather than a terminal singularity.

## 2. Kerr Geometry as Directional Source

A rotating black hole is not isotropic. Its angular momentum supplies a natural parent-side axis. If a child universe emerges through a Kerr-like causal transition, the parent spin axis may be inherited as a large-scale directional memory imprint.

This motivates a link between:

- Kerr spin parameter a/M;
- child-universe anisotropy amplitude;
- CMB hemispherical asymmetry;
- low-l alignment / Axis-of-Evil style anomalies.
