# SoCT Research-to-Product Handoff

## Purpose

This document defines how Sequence of Collapse Theory (SoCT) research outputs may inform EchoGenesis, EchoPath, Vision Codex, or other Echo Labs architecture work without converting speculative or preliminary research into public product claims.

The Sequence-of-collapse-theory repository is the canonical research repository. It owns theory, math scaffolds, empirical hypotheses, experimental proposals, publication sequence, dataset requirements, reproducibility status, and claim boundaries.

Product repositories should not treat SoCT research notes as product certification.

## Boundary principle

```text
SoCT paper / notebook / dataset
  -> reproducibility status
  -> claim boundary
  -> EchoGenesis architecture handoff
  -> product-safe translation
  -> Vision Codex positioning
```

SoCT can inspire architecture. It does not certify product claims.

## What SoCT may provide

SoCT may provide:

- theoretical primitives,
- mathematical scaffolds,
- empirical hypotheses,
- proposed tests,
- toy simulations,
- exploratory analyses,
- preregistration protocols,
- literature positioning,
- publication drafts,
- dataset requirement lists,
- reproducibility status summaries.

## What SoCT does not provide by itself

SoCT does not by itself provide:

- public product proof,
- clinical or safety validation,
- validated hardware performance claims,
- confirmation of new physics,
- confirmation of consciousness-driven collapse,
- confirmation that memory fields physically exist,
- certification that EchoPath or EchoGenesis products work because SoCT is true.

## Required handoff filter

Before any SoCT-derived concept appears in product-facing language, it must pass through this filter:

1. **Source identification** — identify the paper, notebook, dataset, preregistration, or theory note.
2. **Reproducibility status** — mark as imported, partial, blocked, preliminary, preregistered, reproduced, or publication-ready.
3. **Claim boundary** — state what the result supports and what it does not support.
4. **Architecture translation** — convert the concept into an engineering primitive without relying on unproven physics.
5. **Product-safe language** — rewrite claims in practical terms that do not overstate empirical status.
6. **Review owner** — identify whether the output belongs in SoCT, EchoGenesis, EchoPath, Vision Codex, or another repo.

## Product-safe translation examples

Allowed in product repos when technically implemented:

- adaptive memory,
- spatial memory,
- event memory,
- replay-derived tuning,
- persistence,
- field-inspired routing,
- topology-aware diagnostics,
- coherence-inspired stability score,
- threshold-based response,
- memory-biased simulation behavior.

Not allowed as public product claims unless independently reproduced and documented:

- proven new physics,
- verified memory fields,
- dark matter replacement,
- consciousness-driven collapse,
- quantum hardware validation,
- biological equivalence,
- experimentally confirmed Source/Origin dynamics,
- product performance guaranteed by SoCT.

## Repository ownership

```text
Sequence-of-collapse-theory
  Owns: theory, math, empirical tracks, preregistrations, datasets, reproducibility, claim boundaries.

EchoGenesis
  Owns: architecture spine, construct map, system contracts, cross-product conceptual integration.

EchoPath Memory Layer / EchoPath XR
  Owns: product surfaces, demos, developer APIs, public-safe adaptive memory language.

Echo Labs Vision Codex
  Owns: public positioning, narrative strategy, brand language, investor/partner framing.
```

## North star

SoCT should remain scientifically credible by keeping research claims inside the research repository until they are reproducible, bounded, and translated safely. Product work can use the inspiration without inheriting unproven empirical claims.
