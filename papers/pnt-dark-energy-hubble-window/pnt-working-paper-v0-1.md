**Abstract**

Planck Nucleation Theory (PNT) proposes that the SoCT memory field — previously introduced as a phenomenological term in the Shephard-Mirrowen Hamiltonian — has a concrete physical substrate in the structure of spacetime at the Planck scale. The central claim is that virtual particle pair production is the macroscopic signature of continuous Planck-scale black hole nucleation attempts, that the Heisenberg uncertainty window governs their re-absorption timescale, and that the integrated thermal exhaust of these failed nucleations constitutes the observed dark energy density. Successful nucleations — those exceeding the re-absorption threshold — correspond to astrophysical black holes and spawn causally separated child universes. The SoCT memory field is identified as the entanglement residue accumulated in the Planck lattice by both successful and failed nucleation events. This substrate provides first-principles derivations for: (1) the origin and baseline of the memory field term in H_SOC, (2) a physical mechanism for the Causal Inversion Hypothesis junction surface S_uv, (3) a natural interpretation of the Engramon as a persistent Planck cell state modification, (4) a candidate mechanism for observed dark energy, and (5) a void-versus-filament dark energy density differential that is independently testable.

**1. Introduction: Why PNT Is Needed**

The Sequence of Collapse Theory proposes that reality is structured by collapse events and the memory they leave behind. In prior formulations, this was expressed phenomenologically through a memory field term in an extended Hamiltonian:

**H_SOC = H_free + H_loc + λ_M M(x,t) O_M + λ_c Φ_c(x,t) O_c**

This equation successfully separates standard dynamics, localization, memory coupling, and observer-state coupling. However, it leaves an open physical question:

> What is the substrate of M(x,t)?

Planck Nucleation Theory is proposed as a candidate answer. It interprets spacetime not as smooth emptiness but as a dynamic Planck-scale substrate continuously undergoing microscopic nucleation attempts. Most attempts fail and return to the accessible vacuum. Some succeed, creating black holes or causally separated domains. Each attempt leaves a memory residue.

PNT therefore gives a physical interpretation to the memory term rather than leaving it as a purely phenomenological placeholder.

**2. Core PNT Claims**

PNT begins with four linked claims.

**2.1 Planck-scale nucleation attempts**

At the Planck scale, the vacuum continuously attempts to nucleate black-hole-like microstates. These are not ordinary particles but gravitationally extreme fluctuation events.

**2.2 Failed nucleations appear as virtual-particle behavior**

Most nucleation attempts fail within the uncertainty window and are reabsorbed. Their macroscopic signature is interpreted as virtual particle pair production and vacuum fluctuation structure.

**2.3 Successful nucleations become black holes**

If a nucleation exceeds the reabsorption threshold, it becomes gravitationally stable. In macroscopic form, this corresponds to black-hole formation.

**2.4 All nucleations leave memory residue**

Both failed and successful nucleation attempts modify the Planck substrate. The integrated record of these modifications is the SoCT memory field.

**3. Failed Nucleations and Dark Energy**

The key cosmological proposal is that failed nucleation attempts generate an exhaust or residue. This is not ordinary matter and not radiation in the standard sense. It is the low-energy return of Planck-scale events that fail to become stable causal separations.

A first phenomenological expression is:

**ρ_DE^PNT(t) = ∫ S_fail(t') K_exhaust(t - t') dt'**

Where:

- **S_fail(t')** is the failed nucleation source rate.
- **K_exhaust** is the exhaust decay/thermalization kernel.
- **ρ_DE^PNT** is the effective dark energy contribution.

This form naturally connects to the later two-timescale decomposition:

**ρ_DE^PNT(z) = ρ_prompt(z) + ρ_memory(z)**

The prompt channel may generate early-dark-energy-like behavior, while the long-lived memory channel contributes to late-time dark-energy-like behavior.

**4. Successful Nucleations and Child Universes**

Successful nucleations are interpreted as black-hole formation events. In the parent universe, the event appears as gravitational collapse. Across the causal boundary, it may appear as a child-universe origin.

This connects PNT to the Causal Inversion Hypothesis:

- The black-hole interior is not a terminal singularity.
- The boundary acts as a causal transition surface.
- Parent-side collapse and child-side expansion may be coordinate-dual descriptions of one event.

PNT supplies the Planck substrate mechanism that CIH geometrically describes.

**5. The Engramon Interpretation**

The Engramon is proposed as a persistent Planck-cell memory modification. It is not introduced as a confirmed particle. It is a scale or unit of memory imprint in the substrate.

In this formulation:

- failed nucleations create transient exhaust and weak memory residues;
- successful nucleations create stronger topological/causal imprints;
- repeated events accumulate into M(x,t);
- macroscopic effects may emerge when the memory density becomes large or coherent.

**6. Void-Filament Differential Prediction**

If failed nucleation exhaust and memory residue are environment-dependent, the effective dark-energy contribution may differ by cosmic environment.

A working prediction is:

> Void regions may exhibit a higher effective dark-energy or expansion contribution than filament regions.

This motivates the Pantheon+ environment-H0 analysis and the DESI-facing void/filament track.

**7. Relationship to SPARC and Memory-Field Gravity**

The PNT memory residue channel is not limited to cosmology. It is also a candidate substrate for the memory-field gravity track.

The P1 phenomenological relation:

**G_eff(x,t) = G_0 [1 + α M(x,t)]**

can be interpreted under PNT as a macroscopic response to accumulated Planck-substrate memory.

This does not prove P1. It provides a possible physical substrate for the term P1 attempts to constrain observationally.

**8. Open Issues**

The theory requires several unresolved derivations before claim escalation:

1. Derive S_fail(t) from first principles rather than fit it phenomenologically.
2. Determine whether failed-nucleation exhaust can obey CMB/BAO/SN constraints.
3. Derive the plasma transfer function for early prompt exhaust.
4. Connect memory residue to measurable gravitational phenomenology.
5. Clarify whether Engramons are discrete, quasi-discrete, or purely effective units.
6. Constrain PNT parameters using SPARC, Pantheon+, DESI, CMB, and gravitational-wave data.

**9. Claim Boundary**

Use:

> PNT is a speculative physical-substrate proposal for the SoCT memory field, with testable implications for dark energy, black-hole transitions, and memory-field gravity.

Avoid:

> PNT proves dark energy is failed black-hole nucleation or proves that black holes create universes.
